# Data Privacy & GDPR Compliance Policy

**Document Type:** Compliance Policy
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024

## Executive Summary

This policy outlines Skyro's approach to data privacy and compliance with:
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - EU
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) - California
- Other applicable state and federal privacy laws

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All customer personal data collected by Skyro
- All employees with access to customer data
- All third-party vendors processing data on behalf of Skyro
- All geographic locations where Skyro operates

## Data Collection Principles

### 1. Lawful Basis for Processing

We collect and process personal data only when we have a lawful basis:

**Consent:**
- Marketing communications
- Optional product features
- Cookies (non-essential)

**Contract Performance:**
- Account opening and management
- Transaction processing
- Customer support

**Legal Obligation:**
- KYC/AML compliance
- Tax reporting
- Regulatory audits

**Legitimate Interest:**
- Fraud prevention
- Service improvements
- Security monitoring

### 2. Data Minimization

We collect only data that is:
- Necessary for stated purpose
- Adequate for intended use
- Relevant to business need
- Not excessive

**Example - Account Opening:**
Collected: Name, DOB, SSN, Address, Email, Phone
NOT Collected: Race, religion, health data, political views

### 3. Purpose Limitation

Personal data collected for one purpose cannot be used for unrelated purposes without new consent.

**Allowed:**
- Data collected for account opening → Used for fraud detection (compatible purpose)

**Not Allowed:**
- Data collected for account opening → Sold to third parties for marketing (incompatible)

## Data Subject Rights (GDPR)

### Right to Access (Article 15)
Customers can request:
- What data we hold
- How we use it
- Who we share it with
- How long we keep it

**Response Time:** 30 days
**Method:** Self-service portal or support ticket

### Right to Rectification (Article 16)
Customers can correct inaccurate data.

**Implementation:**
- In-app profile editing
- Support ticket for complex changes
- Verification required for sensitive fields

### Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)
Customers can request deletion when:
- Data no longer necessary
- Consent withdrawn
- Objection to processing
- Data processed unlawfully

**Exceptions (we can refuse):**
- Legal obligation (e.g., 7-year financial record retention)
- Legal defense
- Public interest

### Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
Customers can receive their data in machine-readable format (JSON, CSV).

**Included:**
- Transaction history
- Account information
- Profile data

**Excluded:**
- Derived/inferred data (fraud scores, credit risk)
- Data about other people

### Right to Object (Article 21)
Customers can object to:
- Direct marketing (absolute right)
- Profiling
- Automated decision-making

### Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)
Customers can request temporary halt to processing while:
- Accuracy is being verified
- Lawfulness is being assessed

## CCPA Consumer Rights (California)

California residents have additional rights:

### Right to Know
- Categories of personal information collected
- Sources of data
- Business purpose for collection
- Categories of third parties we share with

### Right to Delete
Similar to GDPR erasure, with same exceptions.

### Right to Opt-Out of Sale
**Note:** Skyro does NOT sell personal information.

### Right to Non-Discrimination
We cannot discriminate against users who exercise their rights.

## Data Processing Records

We maintain detailed records of:
- Data processing activities
- Categories of data
- Purpose of processing
- Data retention periods
- Security measures
- Third-party processors

## International Data Transfers

### EU to US Transfers
We use EU-approved mechanisms:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Adequacy decisions where applicable
- Additional safeguards (encryption, access controls)

### Data Localization
- EU customer data stored in EU data centers
- US customer data stored in US data centers
- Cross-border transfers logged and monitored

## Data Retention

### Account Data
- Active accounts: Retained while account open
- Closed accounts: 7 years (financial regulation requirement)
- After 7 years: Anonymized or deleted

### Transaction Data
- 7 years (IRS requirement)
- After 7 years: Anonymized for analytics

### Marketing Data
- Until consent withdrawn + 30 days
- Unsubscribe = immediate cessation

### Logs and Monitoring
- Security logs: 1 year
- Application logs: 90 days
- Audit logs: 7 years

## Third-Party Data Processors

All vendors must:
- Sign Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Implement appropriate security measures
- Not use data for own purposes
- Delete data upon contract termination
- Allow audits

**Current Processors:**
- Plaid (bank account verification) - DPA signed
- Alloy (identity verification) - DPA signed
- SendGrid (email) - DPA signed
- Twilio (SMS) - DPA signed
- AWS (infrastructure) - BAA + DPA

## Security Measures

### Technical Controls
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Access controls (RBAC)
- Multi-factor authentication
- Regular penetration testing

### Organizational Controls
- Data privacy training (annual)
- Background checks for employees
- Confidentiality agreements
- Incident response plan
- Privacy by design in new features

## Data Breach Response

### Breach Definition
Unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of personal data.

### Response Timeline
- **Detection to containment:** <1 hour
- **Assessment:** <4 hours
- **Notification to DPO:** <12 hours
- **Regulatory notification:** <72 hours (if required)
- **Customer notification:** As soon as practical

### Notification Triggers
We notify regulators if breach:
- Likely to result in risk to rights and freedoms
- Affects >500 individuals
- Involves sensitive data (SSN, financial)

## Privacy by Design

All new products/features must:
- Complete Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)
- Minimize data collection
- Enable user controls
- Default to privacy-friendly settings
- Document legal basis for processing

### PIA Template
1. What data is collected?
2. Why is it necessary?
3. What is the lawful basis?
4. How long will it be retained?
5. Who will have access?
6. What are the risks?
7. How are risks mitigated?

## Children's Privacy (COPPA)

- Skyro services are NOT directed at children under 13
- We do NOT knowingly collect data from children under 13
- If discovered, we delete immediately and notify parents

**Age Verification:**
- Date of birth collected during signup
- Accounts with age <18 flagged for review
